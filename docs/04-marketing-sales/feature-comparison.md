# Feature Comparison

## Purpose

This document defines how the product should compare the Starter, Pro, and Agency plans in a way that is commercially clear, credible, and easy to convert on.

It is not only a pricing table input.  
It is also a positioning tool.

The goal is to make each plan feel:

- clear
- justified
- easy to choose
- aligned with a real buyer type

The biggest risk with pricing is not price resistance.  
It is confusion.

If buyers do not understand why the higher plan exists, they will either:

- default to the cheapest one
- hesitate
- or leave

So this document is designed to create a clean upgrade path.

---

### Starter

### Pro

For founders who want the best balance of speed, polish, launch-readiness, and built-in value.

### Agency

For agencies, consultants, and teams who want broader usage rights and better ROI across multiple products or client projects.

---

# 1. Decision Principle

The plans should not be separated by fake filler.

They should be separated by:

- speed
- polish
- launch-readiness
- breadth of included assets
- commercial rights
- support level
- confidence and convenience

That means:

- Starter = strong foundation
- Pro = stronger outcome
- Agency = broader usage and higher leverage

# 3. Plan-by-Plan Sales Framing

## Starter

### Headline

**Start from a modern SaaS foundation**

### Positioning

Starter is for buyers who want the core product foundation at the lowest entry price.

They are comfortable customizing more of the product themselves and want access to the essential codebase, setup, and structure.

### Best fit

- solo founders
- indie hackers
- developers comfortable building on top of a starter
- buyers who want the strongest value at the lowest price

### What Starter really sells

- speed versus starting from zero
- modern stack without setup pain
- pragmatic structure without enterprise overhead
- a clean base to adapt

### Buyer mindset

“I do not need every extra asset. I want the foundation and I can take it from there.”

---

## Pro

### Headline

**The fastest path from starter to launch**

### Positioning

Pro is for buyers who do not just want the foundation.  
They want a stronger launch package, more polish, more ready-made value, and less setup friction.

### Best fit

- founders launching a real product soon
- buyers who care about polish
- builders who want less assembly work
- buyers who want the best overall value

### What Pro really sells

- time saved
- launch readiness
- stronger product presentation
- lower effort between purchase and shipping

### Buyer mindset

“I want the version that gets me closer to launch immediately.”

### Why Pro should win

Pro should be the easiest plan to justify because it turns the product from “good codebase” into “better launch package.”

That is why Pro should be the commercial center of gravity.

---

## Agency

### Headline

**Built for repeat delivery and broader usage**

### Positioning

Agency is for teams, consultants, and studios who want to turn the starter into a leveraged commercial asset across multiple client or internal builds.

### Best fit

- consultants
- agencies
- product studios
- technical teams shipping multiple projects

### What Agency really sells

- broader rights
- better ROI across multiple projects
- a stronger commercial usage fit
- reduced licensing friction

### Buyer mindset

“If I am going to reuse this across client work, I want the plan that matches that reality.”

---

# 4. Why the Upgrade Path Works

## Starter to Pro

The upgrade from Starter to Pro is driven by:

- less setup effort
- more polish
- more launch-ready assets
- more included value
- higher confidence

This is the most important upgrade path in the pricing ladder.

## Pro to Agency

The upgrade from Pro to Agency is driven by:

- broader usage rights
- multi-client or multi-project use
- commercial leverage
- better fit for service businesses

This path is narrower, but higher value.

# 7. Comparison Messaging Rules

## Rule 1

Do not make Starter feel weak.

Starter must still feel like a real product with real value.

## Rule 2

Do make Pro feel smarter.

Pro should feel like the best trade-off, not just “more stuff.”

## Rule 3

Do not overload Agency with fake extras.

Agency should mostly sell on rights, leverage, and business fit.

## Rule 4

Do not invent plan differences the repo cannot support.

The comparison must stay grounded in what is actually included or realistically includable.

# 9. Objection Handling by Plan

## Starter objection

**“Is the cheaper plan still enough?”**

Answer:
Yes. Starter includes the real core foundation. It is not a crippled teaser. It is the right choice for developers who are comfortable customizing more on their own.

## Pro objection

**“Why should I pay more?”**

Answer:
Because Pro reduces time-to-launch more aggressively. It is for buyers who want more polish, more ready-made value, and less assembly work.

## Agency objection

**“Why not just buy Pro?”**

Answer:
If you are using the product across client work or multiple builds, Agency better matches the way you create value from the starter.

# 12. Suggested Website Comparison Labels

Use labels like:

- Core foundation
- Included
- Expanded
- Agency license
- Launch-ready extras
- Broader usage rights

Avoid labels like:

- Basic
- Advanced
- Enterprise

Why:
Those labels push the product mentally toward software subscriptions or corporate tooling rather than a premium starter product.
