# FAQ

## Purpose
Handle the objections that slow down conversion for paid starter products.

## FAQ draft

### Why is this not a 49-dollar boilerplate?
Because the goal is not to sell a disposable template. The value is in a modern stack, monetization-oriented foundations, better structure, and less time wasted rebuilding the same setup from scratch.

### Who is this for?
Solo founders, indie builders, consultants, and small technical teams who want to launch a SaaS product faster without adopting a mini-framework first.

### Who is this not for?
Teams looking for a heavy enterprise architecture, deep internal platform abstractions, or a plug-in ecosystem on day one.

### Is this beginner-friendly?
It is best suited to developers who are already comfortable with Next.js and want to move faster with a better foundation.

### Can I customize it easily?
Yes. The product is meant to be easier to understand and modify than overbuilt starters. The goal is fast adaptation, not internal-framework complexity.

### Does it include billing?
It includes the billing foundation and Stripe-ready direction needed for a SaaS starter. The landing page and docs should describe the exact included billing flows clearly.

### Does it include team or workspace support?
Only claim the exact level that is shipped and documented. Do not imply a full multi-tenant product unless the repo clearly proves it.

### Why should I buy this instead of building it myself?
You can build it yourself. The real question is whether rebuilding auth, billing foundations, setup, and app structure is the best use of your next 40 to 80 hours.

### Will I get updates?
Yes, but the policy should be explicitly stated by plan. Keep it simple and avoid vague lifetime support promises.

### Can I use it for client work?
Yes on the Agency or Extended plan. Keep licensing language explicit so buyers do not need to guess.
