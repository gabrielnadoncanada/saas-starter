# Ideal Customer Profile

## Purpose
Define who the starter is for, what they value, and what drives or blocks purchase.

## Primary ICP
Technical founder, indie hacker, or solo developer launching a SaaS product with urgency and limited tolerance for foundational boilerplate.

## Secondary ICP
Small product team or agency that repeatedly launches internal or client-facing SaaS products and wants a reusable, monetization-ready base.

## Not the customer
- beginners who cannot work comfortably in Next.js,
- enterprise teams needing heavy compliance or custom infrastructure,
- buyers who want a no-code product,
- buyers seeking maximum flexibility across every app category.

## Buyer profile
- comfortable with JavaScript/TypeScript and modern React ecosystems,
- values speed, leverage, and clean defaults,
- wants to avoid rebuilding auth, billing, roles, onboarding, and admin basics,
- usually shipping alone or with 1 to 3 collaborators.

## Buyer goals
- launch faster,
- reduce setup and architecture decisions,
- avoid beginner mistakes in billing, auth, and app structure,
- start from something trustworthy and modifiable,
- look credible early with a polished dashboard and landing page.

## Main frustrations
- boilerplate fatigue,
- messy starters with hidden conventions,
- documentation that is incomplete or shallow,
- templates that look good but break under real use,
- too many moving parts before first revenue.

## Buying triggers
- starting a new SaaS idea,
- rebuilding a weak internal boilerplate,
- wanting to launch quickly after market validation,
- needing Stripe, auth, dashboard, and database integration immediately.

## Purchase objections
- "Will I understand the code quickly?"
- "Is this too opinionated?"
- "Can I remove what I do not need?"
- "Is the documentation strong enough?"
- "Is this just another pretty template?"

## Decision criteria
1. clarity of architecture,
2. included monetization primitives,
3. quality of docs and setup flow,
4. UI credibility,
5. confidence that customization will be fast.

## Expected proof
- live demo,
- screenshots of real app states,
- setup docs preview,
- architecture overview,
- concrete list of included modules,
- examples of what can be customized in minutes.

## Desired transformation
From "I need to spend days or weeks rebuilding the foundation" to "I can focus on my differentiator immediately."

## Open questions
- Which ICP has stronger willingness to pay: solo founders or micro-agencies?
- Should messaging prioritize launch speed, monetization readiness, or AI-native capabilities?
