# Tenviq — Beta Feedback Outreach ICP

## Goal
Land 50 honest async written reviews of the Tenviq starter from qualified Next.js devs, in exchange for lifetime access.

## The prospect — one sentence
A technical indie hacker or small-team founder who has shipped (or is actively building) a Next.js SaaS and has evaluated or used at least one competing starter.

## Hard qualifiers (ALL must be true)
- Ships in Next.js (App Router preferred) — visible from tweets, GitHub, or portfolio
- Currently building OR has launched a SaaS / paid product in the last 18 months
- Active publicly in the last 60 days (tweet, GitHub commit, IH post, blog)
- Can write English feedback at a technical level
- Reachable via Twitter DM, Indie Hackers DM, GitHub profile email, or personal site

## Soft qualifiers (nice-to-have — boost priority)
- Mentioned ShipFast / Makerkit / Supastarter / Saasfly / next-forge / Shipixen in the last 6 months
- Pain tweets about auth, billing, multi-tenant, AI integration boilerplate
- Has a public "building in public" thread or launch
- Uses shadcn/ui, Prisma, Stripe, or better-auth already (stack overlap = credible reviewer)
- Following indie hacker voices: @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @shadcn, @dan_abramov, @leerob

## Disqualifiers (skip immediately)
- No shipped product AND no public build activity → too junior to give useful feedback
- Pure content creator / educator with no product of their own
- Someone already selling a competing starter → conflict of interest
- Follower-count farmers with generic AI-slop bios
- Accounts private or inactive >3 months
- Students / "learning Next.js" accounts — great people, wrong audience for *review*

## Qualification signal — the 30-second check
Open their profile and look for ONE of:
1. A product URL in bio that loads a real SaaS
2. A pinned tweet announcing a launch (past 12 months)
3. A GitHub repo with >20 commits in the last 90 days on a Next.js SaaS
4. An IH product page with revenue or launch badge

If none → skip. If ≥1 → add to prospects.csv.

## Why this ICP
These people can evaluate DX meaningfully because they've felt the pain Tenviq solves. They also have *taste* (they picked frameworks, they critique) — their feedback is signal, not noise. And many of them have audiences, so a great review becomes a testimonial or word-of-mouth post-launch.

## What we are NOT doing (and why)
- **Not targeting agencies/consultancies** — they want client work, not lifetime starter access
- **Not targeting CTOs at funded startups** — too busy, feedback too "enterprise"
- **Not mass-DMing** — personalized only, 10-15/day max to avoid spam flags
- **Not offering cash** — attracts mercenaries, not real signal
