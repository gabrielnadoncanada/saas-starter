# Message templates — EN

**Rules for every message:**
- Under 500 chars. Twitter DMs get skimmed — long = ignored.
- ONE specific reference to them (a tweet, a repo, a launch). Copy-pastable hooks = instant skip.
- ONE ask. Feedback, that's it. No call, no signup form, no "jump on a call to align."
- No "hope this finds you well." No "quick question." No "saw you're a founder."
- Signature: just your first name.

---

## Variant A — "Shipped a similar thing" hook

Use when: they shipped a SaaS recently and are active on Twitter.

```
Hey {name} — saw your launch of {product} last {month}, the {specific_detail} is clean.

I'm about to launch Tenviq, a Next.js 16 starter (better-auth, Prisma, Stripe, multi-tenant, AI assistant baked in). Pre-launch, looking for ~50 builders who've actually shipped to poke at it.

Free lifetime access for 30 min of written feedback. No call, do it async whenever.

Interested? I'll send the repo access.

— {your_first_name}
```

---

## Variant B — "You've complained about this exact thing" hook

Use when: they tweeted pain about auth / billing / boilerplate in last 90 days.

```
{name} — your {date} tweet about {specific_pain} is exactly why I built Tenviq.

Next.js 16 starter with better-auth + Stripe + multi-tenant + AI assistant, no glue code. Pre-launch, giving 50 lifetime accesses to builders who'll send honest written feedback.

You'd be a perfect reviewer because you've felt the pain. 30 min async, no call.

Want in?

— {your_first_name}
```

---

## Variant C — "Starter veteran" hook

Use when: they've publicly used/reviewed ShipFast, Makerkit, Supastarter, next-forge.

```
Hey {name} — saw you {action: used/reviewed/tweeted about} {competitor_starter}. Curious what your take was.

I'm launching Tenviq, another Next.js 16 starter (better-auth, Prisma, Stripe, multi-tenant + AI assistant). Would love an honest comparison take from someone who's already kicked the tires on others.

Free lifetime access for 30 min of written feedback. Async, no call.

Repo access if you're in.

— {your_first_name}
```

---

## Follow-up #1 — D+4 if no reply

```
No pressure {name} — just bumping this in case it buried. Still have a slot if you want it.
```

That's it. One line. More than that = needy.

---

## Follow-up #2 — D+10 if no reply

```
Last nudge — closing the beta list Friday. If you want lifetime access, just reply "in" and I'll send the link.
```

Then never again. Two follow-ups max.

---

## Post-acceptance — access + brief

Send this the moment they say yes:

```
Nice. Access: {repo_link or download_link}.

Feedback brief (answer whichever hit):
1. First 30 min — what confused you, what worked?
2. Anything you'd rip out or rename?
3. What's missing that you'd expect in a 2026 Next.js SaaS starter?
4. Would you ship your next SaaS on this? Why / why not?

Reply in a Notion doc, Loom, gist, whatever. No deadline — but the sooner the better since I'm iterating live.

Thanks for doing this.

— {your_first_name}
```

---

## Anti-patterns — DO NOT

- Start with "Hope you're well" / "Hi there" / "Quick question"
- Use the word "reach out" / "touch base" / "circle back"
- Pitch features in the opening line
- Mention that you sent the same DM to 50 people
- Attach a PDF or link to a 2-min demo video in DM #1 (lowers reply rate — save for after yes)
- Use emojis in the first message (reads as mass DM)
- Send on weekends or Monday morning — Tue-Thu 9am-2pm their timezone hits best
